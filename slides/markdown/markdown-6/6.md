# Description

▶ Cox, Ross, Rubinstein, 1979.

▶ One risky asset : $d = 1$ .

▶ Riskless asset : S 0t = (1 + r )t , t = 0, . . . , T with $r \geq 0$ .

▶ Risky asset : $( S _ { t } ) _ { t = 0 , \ldots , T } .$

Returns at t = 1, . . . , T :

$$
R _ {t} ^ {0} := \frac {S _ {t} ^ {0} - S _ {t - 1} ^ {0}}{S _ {t - 1} ^ {0}} = r \quad \text {a n d} \quad R _ {t} := \frac {S _ {t} - S _ {t - 1}}{S _ {t - 1}}.
$$

▶ Assumption : $\mathbb { P } [ R _ { t } = \{ d , u \} ] = 1$ for t = 1, . . . , T , where−1 < d < u.

# No-arbitrage and completeness

# Theorem

The binomial model is arbitrage-free iff $d < r < u .$

# Theorem

If $d < r < u$ , the binomial model is complete and there exists a uniqueequivalent martingale measure $\mathbb { Q }$ which is defined as follows : R1, . . . , RTare i.i.d. under Q s.t.

$$
\mathbb {Q} [ R _ {t} = u ] = \frac {r - d}{u - d} := q \in (0, 1), \quad t = 1, \dots , T.
$$

In particular, $( S _ { t } ) _ { t = 0 , \ldots , T }$ is a Markov process under Q.

# Structure of Complete Market

Consider a T −period market which contains one riskless asset and d riskyassets and is modeled on a probability space $( \Omega , \mathcal { F } , \mathbb { P } )$ .

# Proposition

Assume that there exists an unique martingale measure (the market isarbitrage-free and complete). Then there exists $d + 1$ adapted processes$\{ \delta _ { t } ^ { i }$ , t ∈ {0, . . . , T − 1}}, $i \in \{ 1 , \ldots , d + 1 \}$ , taking values in Rd such that

$$
\sum_ {i = 1} ^ {d + 1} \mathbb {P} \left(S _ {t} - S _ {t - 1} = \delta_ {t - 1} ^ {j} \mid \mathcal {F} _ {t - 1}\right) = 1 \mathbb {P} \text {- a . s .} \forall t \in \{1, \dots , T \}.
$$

Moreover for all t, the family of vectors $\{ \delta _ { t } ^ { 1 } , \dots , \delta _ { t } ^ { d + 1 } \}$ is affinallytindependent P-a.s. i.e. the family of vectors $\{ \delta _ { t } ^ { 2 } - \delta _ { t } ^ { 1 } , \ldots , \delta _ { t } ^ { d + 1 } - \delta _ { t } ^ { 1 } \}$ arelinearly independent $\mathbb { P }$ -p.s.

▶ In particular, the arbitrage-free and complete non-trivial model for$d = 1$ must be the binomial model. Here we call a model non-trivial if$\mathbb { P } [ S _ { t } = S _ { t - 1 } ] < 1$ for all $t = 1 , \ldots , T .$ .

▶ Except from tree models where the number of branches is equal to thenumber of (independent) assets, discrete time models are incomplete.
