\chapter*{Introduction}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Introduction}
This document serves as a comprehensive study guide for the course "Stochastic Finance and Risk Modelling". It expands upon the lecture slides relative to Chapter 3: "One-period model of financial market".

In this chapter, we introduce the fundamental concepts of financial modeling in a simplified one-period setting. We define the market, assets (riskless and risky), and portfolios. We then explore the crucial concept of arbitrage and the Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing (FTAP), which links the absence of arbitrage to the existence of risk-neutral measures. Finally, we discuss contingent claims (derivatives), their pricing, and market completeness.
